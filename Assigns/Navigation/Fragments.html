Navigation in Android

Unit 2: Fragments

Much of this path centers on fragments -- a means of changing what looks like Activities, without the heavy overhead of starting and stopping full processes.

  1. Consider the property declaration that appears in both fragments: val binding get() = _binding!!

    • What is the role of the variable _binding? (A Kotlin language term)

    • Why do we need the !! operator? What would we need to do to make the property compile if we removed the !!? What are we counting on the user of the Fragment not to do?

  1. The third parameter of inflate in both Fragments is left false. What is the purpose of this parameter, and why does leaving it false make sense given this is a Fragment?

  2. Why are onCreateView and onCreateOptionsMenu *passed* inflaters as parameters, when in the earlier Activity/Intent based version of the WordsApp there was a menuInflater and a layoutInflater just "available" in OnCreate and OnCreateOptionsMenu? Why could the earlier version just use existing inflaters? Where were they declared that made this possible? Why is it necessary to pass inflaters to the Fragments? What inflaters are being passed?

  3. Why does onCreateOptionsMenu no longer return a boolean as it did in the Activity/Intent version, and why is the passed-in menu now nonnullable? What did a false-return from onCreateOptionsMenu signify in the earlier case? How is that handled for Fragments, and why does this mean the Fragment onCreateOptionsMenu now returns Unit (right?) and takes a nonnullable Menu.

  4. Why is the android:label value in the navigation graph? Shouldn't that be part of the fragment layout itself? When would this possibly-odd decision be useful?

  5. Why don't we do binding operations in onCreate instead of onCreateView?

  6. Why does the navigation graph attach arguments to the target, instead of the action? The arguments are clearly attached to the action in the navigate call.

  7. What is the "letter =" notation in the actionLetterListFragmentToWordListFragment call in LetterAdapter? Is that an assignment statement embedded in the call?

  8. Why do the navigation-specialized tests need ApplicationProvider, while earlier tests e.g. for Quotes, even those that tested the UI, did not? What do you think the TestNavHostController does with the context passed to it? This question is really asking you to understand the structural difference between earlier UI tests and these navigation-specialized tests.

  9. What type of thing does launchFragmentInContainer return? Is the "themeResId =" in the call needed? What would happen if we omitted it? Why?

  10. How many deprecated functions are there in androidx.fragment.app.testing? What does this suggest about the design history of this testing library? How do you feel about the design of the fragment testing library in general?

  11. Write a standard UI test for this application, like those for Quotes and Dogglers. This one should start at the first page, drill down to the "C" words, and verify that C words are now showing.

  12. As with prior exercises, describe one deeper exploration you did springing off of the concepts in this path, which docs you found useful, etc.
